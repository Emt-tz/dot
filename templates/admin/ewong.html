Introduction

Ewong’an traditional tourism will focus on using tourism activities to empower young Maasai women from the age of 12 - 24 who have dropped out of school because of pregnancy and early marriage in Maasai society, specifically in Longido village. These women will be empowered to form entrepreneurs women’s groups that will produce traditional arts and crafts, prepare traditional Maasai experiences such as cuisine, traditional dances and storytelling for tourists.

Vision

We believe that through tourism, societies around the world can be connected and empowered, even the remote and marginalized ones

Mission

Connecting societies through tourism


The Challenge being addressed

The challenge that is addressed is girls who dropout of school are facing financial problems at the same time they have children and family to take care of. Ewong’at will support these girls who have lost their education opportunity to get a livelihood through the tourism industry.


Who is the solution for? Describe your customer

Ewong’at will create a unique and authentic tour and experience of a traditional Maasai Boma and culture for local and foreign tourists. In these Bomas, tourists and these Ewong’an women groups will have the opportunity to meet and interact. Ewong’at will create the link between these women groups and tourists, where the women will sell their products and services to tourists and for tourists to experience Maasai culture in detail with an affordable budget.


What support do you need to bring the innovation to life

Ewong’at needs financial support that will help to establish a unique and traditional tourism BOMA, as well as mentorship and incubation support.
